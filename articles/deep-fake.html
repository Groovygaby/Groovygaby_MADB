Slew of deepfake video adverts of Sunak on Facebook raises alarm over AI risk to election

In a recent and concerning development, Facebook witnessed the posting of over 143 deceptive advertisements that impersonated Rishi Sunak in the lead-up to the elections. These adverts showcased a sophisticated level of deepfake technology. Among them was a fabricated video featuring a BBC newsreader announcing a scandal involving Sunak, falsely accusing him of secretly earning substantial sums from a project originally intended for the general public. Another prominent fake advert suggested that Elon Musk had developed an application capable of monitoring stock market transactions. This was coupled with a doctored clip of Sunak, falsely suggesting that the UK government had opted to test this application instead of risking the financial resources of its citizens.

These misleading adverts did not stop at spreading false information; they also directed viewers to a counterfeit BBC webpage that promoted a fraudulent investment scheme. This incident underscores the growing challenge of fake news in the digital era, particularly its potential to manipulate election outcomes. While the UK government has implemented measures like the Online Safety Act to combat such misinformation, there is growing apprehension among regulators about keeping pace with the rapid advancements in artificial intelligence, especially with the general election scheduled for November.

Meta, the parent company of Facebook, has reported that it has successfully removed the majority of this misleading content. However, the effectiveness of such measures remains a topic of debate.

An analysis conducted by (MIT Technology Review 2023) delves into the complexities of AI's influence on elections. The review suggests that while the risk of AI being used to manipulate electoral outcomes is undeniable, the actual impact of such tactics may not be as straightforward or observable as feared. Elections, by their nature, are intricate events influenced by a multitude of factors, making the direct impact of AI-driven misinformation challenging to quantify. This review sheds light on the ongoing debate about the role of technology in shaping political discourse and the efficacy of current regulations in mitigating AI's potential misuse in the democratic process.

💡Lesson of the day: 💡

We live in uncertain times, filled with deepfakes and misinformation. Elections can raise this uncertainty to new heights, so it is imperative to be sceptical about any political news you consume, especially if it is trying to discredit someone.
