‘Like Christmas’: Liquor stores overrun after fake alcohol ban voice note shakes confidence in government

In August 2022 Anthony Moyneaux from Times Live reported on a widespread rumour that spread through a WhatsApp voicenote. This rumour claimed that the president of South Africa would be reinstating a national alcohol ban. The false claim triggered widespread panic buying throughout the nation.

The government later became aware of this spread of fake news and a minister in parliament debunked the rumour as unfounded. Unfortunately, the damage had been done as many South Africans unwilling to risk being caught unprepared, had already rushed to buy alcohol in large quantities.

Lungi Mtshali, who represents the Department of Cooperative Governance and Traditional Affairs also confirmed to News24 that the rumour had no truth behind it. There were speculations that it might have been spread for the benefit of the alcohol industry.

💡Lesson of the day: 💡

Before letting fear or panic guide your actions, pause and evaluate the reasons behind your response as well as the credibility of the information that triggered such a reaction. By adopting a mindset that values making decisions based on evidence and being sceptical towards unverified claims, you can play a role in creating a society that is better informed and less prone to knee-jerk reactions.
