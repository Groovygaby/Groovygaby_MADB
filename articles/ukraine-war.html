Ukraine war: How TikTok fakes pushed Russian lies to millions

In December 2023 an article published by the BBC uncovered a disinformation campaign that targeted Defense Minister Oleksiy Reznikov and his daughter, Anastasiya. The fabricated news falsely claimed that Reznikov and his family were involved in purchases, including luxury cars and villas abroad which supposedly contributed to his removal from office in September. Anastasiya hinted that these allegations might have played a role in her father’s dismissal although he was also facing accusations of corruption.

During a vacation, Anastasiya’s husband brought this misinformation to her attention. It falsely alleged that she had acquired villas in Madrid and on the French Riviera; however, upon investigation, it was revealed that these properties were actually still for sale and had not been purchased by anyone. This tactic of spreading false information was not limited to the Reznikovs but was part of a broader strategy aimed at various Ukrainian officials.

BBC Verify traced the origins of the misinformation network back to Russia. This conclusion was reached based on linguistic errors commonly made by Russian speakers as well as the analysed videos containing various links to websites associated with Russian disinformation efforts pretending to be Western news outlets.

So, why were they being targeted? A technical research manager from Stanford Internet Observatory analysed the motives behind these targeted disinformation campaigns and suggested that by promoting narratives of corruption within Ukraine, the motive was to undermine support for Ukraine’s ongoing war effort.

Moreover, according to an article titled "The Role of Social Media in the Russia-Ukraine Conflict" written by Najat Al Saied, it is suggested that both Russia and Ukraine utilise social media to shape public perception to their advantage, capitalising on the platforms' ability to echo users' preexisting beliefs. Al Saied argues that the inclination towards relying on media for news consumption arises from a desire for more autonomy and perceived objectivity.

💡Lesson of the day: 💡

It seems that leaders and high-profile individuals often become targets for fake news. When politics comes into play, it's important to scrutinise the underlying intentions in times of intense volatility such as a war.
