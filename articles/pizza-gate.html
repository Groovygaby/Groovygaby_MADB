Pizzagate: Gunman fires in restaurant at centre of conspiracy

Fake news can reduce the impact of real news by competing with it.

In 2016, a bizarre fake news story made the headlines in Washington aiming to discredit the Democratic Party and Hillary Clinton. This occurred during election time, suggesting an intention to sway public opinion against the Democrats. Conspiracists came up with a theory called Pizza Gate and accused a pizzeria linked to the Democratic Party and Hillary Clinton, of being the centre of a child sex trafficking ring. This situation escalated when a man who was misled by these claims, walked into the restaurant with a gun and fired it, leading to his consequent arrest. The false theory was based on Democratic Party emails that had been hacked into. The owner of the restaurant was a Democratic Party donor and was featured in these emails because he was organising a Democratic fundraiser. However, when these emails got leaked, people in online forums started to conspire and suggested innocuous food terms were actually secret codes for illicit activities.

Right-wing blogs supported by Donald Trump propagated this theory. While their intention was clearly aimed at turning voters against the Democratic Party, they may not have fully realised the potential consequences of spreading such false information. The armed man who entered the restaurant posed a danger to the public and could have potentially killed staff and patrons. This was exemplified by Edgar Welch, the man arrested, whose actions were driven by his strong opposition to child pornography and paedophilia which led him to take drastic measures based on the false claims.

Research from Syracuse University supports the idea that deeply held beliefs, when echoed by misinformation, can motivate individuals to act in ways that are extremely detrimental. A salacious and disturbing topic such as child pornography was bound to attract people's attention and provoke extreme reactions, the creators of this news were well aware of this fact. Fake news is often inflammatory and sensational, dragging people in under a false pretence in order to garner attention and discussion. However, in this instance, it went well beyond discussion, human lives were at risk, and this shows how dangerous spreading fake news can be.

💡Lesson of the day: 💡

Critical thinking is essential. Don't let sensational headlines influence you without digging deeper for the truth. Fake news not only distorts reality but can also incite dangerous situations as displayed above.
