Sachin Tendulkar: Indian cricket legend flags 'disturbing' deepfake video

A fake video was circulated depicting a cricket legend, Sachin Tendulkar, supposedly promoting an online gaming app as an easy way to make money. It also falsely claimed that his daughter had used it recently. It has later been found that many Bollywood actresses have been targeted in a similar way.

India’s junior IT minister said they will soon enforce strict rules to ensure social media platforms promptly remove deep fake videos. Under India's IT rules, social media platforms must ensure that "no misinformation is posted by any user" and platforms that do not comply could be taken to court under Indian law.

As celebrities are often aligned with a brand and are revered, people have faith and trust in their endorsements, making them ideal targets for such schemes. The Wall Street Journal highlighted this issue on 5 October 2022 in an article titled “Deepfakes of Celebrities Have Begun Appearing in Ads, With or Without Their Permission. The article states that this deceptive scheme creates videos that use computer-generated renditions to make it look as though celebrities have said or done things that they have not. This tactic is partly employed to reduce costs. For the celebrity in question, it poses a significant challenge to go after each and every small business or person who does this to them.

According to the Hindustan Times, India ranks as the 6th most vulnerable country to be targeted in this way with South Korea in first place. While many celebrities and high-profile people are falsely represented, there are also public figures like Donald Trump who have helped create and promote their own fake news.

💡Lesson of the day: 💡

The classic parental saying “If your friend jumped off a cliff, would you?” is very pertinent in this story. Although you may look up to celebrities, don’t believe everything you see and hear from them. If their content seems sensational, urgent or out of character, fact-check it before believing it.
