Who's behind Israel-Gaza disinformation and hate online?

This article published in October 2023 explores the widespread dissemination of fake news, conspiracy theories and hateful content on social media regarding the Israeli invasion of Gaza. It expands on a scary realisation that many of the social media accounts spreading hateful content are fake.

Shocking videos and messages were and still are being spread. The disinformation in many cases, was not limited to accounts trying to undermine violence against hostages. There have been many profiles supporting Israel’s actions and sharing misleading and hateful content.

By distorting and confusing things online, it becomes harder to get the real truth of what is happening if individuals rely solely on social media for updates on the crisis. As Marianna Spring who wrote the article points out, this can have serious implications for the international community when it comes to investigating allegations of war crimes, providing aid and accurately understanding the developments.

Cyabra, an Israel-based social media analytics firm, has found that approximately 20% of accounts discussing Hamas's attacks are fictitious. These accounts may be automated or operated by individuals under false pretences.

Spring offers certain clues that can serve as indicators of an account’s authenticity. One suggestion is to check whether a profiler has recently been created and is rapidly sharing a lot of divisive and or conflicting content, this suggests that the account is probably inauthentic. However, confirming the falseness of a profile and identifying its creators is challenging without cooperation from social media platforms, which are often reluctant to share such data.

💡Lesson of the day: 💡

It is important to remember that social media should not be considered a reliable source of information in all cases. News publications may not be perfect either, but they tend to be more accurate. Countries at war with each other are most likely both using social media to spread fake news, making it crucial for you to seek alternative sources in order to achieve a clearer understanding of the conflict.
