India WhatsApp 'child kidnap' rumours claim two more victims

In June 2018 the BBC reported on a tragic incident that occurred due to a circulation of misinformed opinions being shared on Whatsapp. This story involved the tragic deaths of an audio engineer and a digital artist who were mistakenly believed to be child kidnappers and were fatally assaulted after stopping to ask for directions.

This false accusation originated from a video that was circulated on WhatsApp, which turned out to be fabricated and not even produced in India. The situation worsened when local media outlets started spreading these rumours, lending them unwarranted credibility. Unfortunately, this was not an isolated incident and officials have not yet been able to identify any incidents of child abduction cases related to these fake stories and videos.

The issue of misinformation leading to violence is particularly severe in India. Annie Gowen, reporting for the Washington Post in 2018, highlighted the issue of mob violence triggered by baseless messages on WhatsApp. She noted that India has a vast number of digitally illiterate smartphone users, comprising over 200 million people who send billions of messages daily. This makes India Whatsapp’s largest market and a hotbed for the rapid spread of fake news.

Nikhil Pahwa, a digital rights activist, suggested that in order to mitigate this issue, WhatsApp should require messages to display the original sender’s number on all forwarded messages. This would help with identifying the origins of any potential misinformation. In response to these tragedies, Google has launched a programme in India designed to train journalists on recognising and debunking fake news. This initiative is part of the ongoing endeavour to combat the dissemination of fake news in India.

💡Lesson of the day: 💡

Be wary of messages that encourage violence in any form. Creators of fake news often use mob mentality to entice people. Consequently, individuality and personal beliefs will be stripped away, potentially leading a person to make decisions or take actions that are out of character.
