Gwyneth Paltrow's Goop series on Netflix slammed by NHS chief

In January 2020 the BBC reported on a story about an NHS chief criticising a wellness brand that was disseminating fake news. Gwyneth Paltrow, a well-known actress featured in a Netflix documentary called Goop. The documentary focused on her health and wellness brand, offering unsolicited recommendations throughout the show.

Simon Stevens, the NHS Chief Executive in England, criticised Gwyneth Paltrow's wellness brand, Goop, for disseminating false health information. He mentions practices performed by them such as vampire facials and a body worker who claims to cure mental issues by simply moving his hands two inches above a person's body.

Goop also promotes ideas that sunscreen is not good for you and advocates colonic irrigation, DIY coffee enemas and the use of jade and rose quartz eggs for hormonal balance and menstrual cycle regulation when used vaginally. Stevens remarked that while fake news is often associated with politics, the public's natural concern for their health has made Goop an attractive platform for dubious health advice from unqualified individuals.

Consequently, Goop faced legal repercussions from California's consumer protection office due to these unfounded health claims. 💡Lesson of the day: 💡

The proof is in the pudding. If you come across a claim that seems strange or highly unconventional, weigh up the facts first instead of blindly believing it. Consider whether the claim is grounded in legitimate science or if it falls under pseudoscience, where statements and practices may appear effective and factual but lack scientific validity.
