How to Spot Fake News

Learn how to spot fake news with practical tips and tools displayed below, these tips are designed to help you navigate online information in a safe and informed manner.

A Guidebook on How to Spot Fake News:

Examine the Source:

Look at the website's URL where you're getting your information. Mistakes in the URL or unusual domain extensions like ".infonet" or ".offer" could be red flags. If the website is unknown to you, delve into its "About Us" section.

Investigate the Author:

Do some digging to find out if they're reputable. Are they authentic, well-regarded, speaking on topics within their expertise and do they have a bias? Reflect on the potential motivations behind the author's writing.

Consult Additional Sources:

Is the story being covered by other trustworthy media outlets? Are credible references cited in the article? Renowned news organisations adhere to strict fact-checking standards, so wide coverage is a positive indication.

Adopt a Skeptical Mindset:

Be aware that fake news often aims to elicit strong emotional responses. Question the purpose behind the story's creation. Is it pushing a specific viewpoint or trying to lead you to another website?

Verify the Facts:

Legitimate news often includes tons of factual information - data, expert quotes, etc. A lack of these should raise doubts. Be wary of incorrect dates or timelines and check the publication date to ensure relevance.

Review the Comments Section:

Even genuine articles can have misleading comments underneath. Watch out for comments or links that might be generated by bots or people spreading confusion.

Reflect on Personal Biases:

Recognise your biases. Could they be affecting your interpretation of the news? Social media algorithms tend to reinforce our preexisting beliefs or interests, but exposure to varied sources can help in forming balanced views.

Distinguish Satire from Serious Content:

With the popularity of satirical websites, it can sometimes be hard to tell if a story is meant to be humorous. Verify whether the site is known for satire or humorous content.

Assess Image Authenticity:

Manipulated images are common on social media. Look for signs of editing like unnatural warping or inconsistencies in lighting. Utilise tools like Google Reverse Image Search to trace an image's origin and check for alterations.

Consult Fact-Checking Websites:

Some reputable fact-checking sites include PolitiFact, Fact Check and BBC Reality Check. These can be invaluable resources for verifying information.
