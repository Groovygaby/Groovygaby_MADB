What is fake news?

Ever wondered why your social media feed is full of questionable content and sensational headlines? Dive into what fake news really is and how deepfakes are blurring reality as we know it. Let's get smart about what we share by recognising fact from fiction.

Fake News

Fake news is deceptive information masquerading as legitimate news designed to mislead or manipulate readers.

It often spreads rapidly and uses sensationalism or urgency to gain attention.

It can be spread through social media, websites, or even traditional news outlets.

Fake news is created with goals in mind: making money, influencing opinions, or stirring up chaos.

It's important to be critical of the news we read and to check if it comes from a reliable source.

Deepfakes pose a risk as they can mislead people and tarnish reputations.

Deepfakes may be used to produce fake news or disseminate misinformation.

Deepfakes use artificial intelligence (AI) to alter or create videos and images so they seem real

DEEPFAKES

Deep fakes have taken the world by storm and are best known for their ability to create eerily realistic fake videos.

MISINFORMATION

You think something is true but it actually isn’t. E.g. You tell your peers that the deadline is tomorrow because you think it’s been changed, but it hasn’t.

VS

DISINFORMATION

You know something isn’t true, but you tell people anyway. E.g. You know that the deadline isn’t tomorrow but you tell everyone it is to create anxiety.
